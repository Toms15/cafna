Sfogliatella ricciaThis world-famous Neapolitan speciality is known as “riccia” or “curly” because of its case of golden waves of handmade phyllo pastry, filled with a fragrant cream of sweetened ricotta, semolina, natural candied peel and cinnamon.

€ 1.50

Sfogliatella frollaAn alternative to the sfogliatella riccia, which has a crispy shell, this version is softer and simpler. A round short-crust pastry shell, filled with the same blend of ricotta, semolina, sugar, natural candied peel and cinnamon.

€ 1.50

PastierinaA traditional Easter pie, associated with the goddess Ceres and rebirth, it has now become a year-round classic. Its crunchy short-crust case contrasts with a soft filling of masterfully-blended ricotta, candied peel and cooked wheat.

€ 1.50

CapresinaThe Caprese cake is found not just on Capri, but throughout the Sorrento Peninsula and the Amalfi Coast. Its soft dough is made from bitter cocoa, extra dark chocolate and sweet ground almonds.

€ 1.50
